Time-Series Forecasting Project

Project Overview

This project explores the concept of time-series forecasting and how it can be used to effectively predict trends for future data based on existing data. To show this, I decided to use data based on a topic that I am passionate about, which is the lack of a presence of some European soccer leagues in the United States. At the end, I use the forecasts to argue which leagues should try to increase their influence in the United States both for economic and popularity gain.

Objectives and Methodologies

The objective of this project was to accurately provide forecasts of the search volume statistics for each league. To obtain existing data to base this off of, I used Goggle Trends. I decided to a five-year period to account for the popularities both before and after COVID to try and provide a general prediction. Although many time-series forecasting methods are available, I decided to use the SARIMAX method to effectively account for the seasonality of the data, which occurs due to each league having a two to three month offseason. I also used grid search to obtain the best possible combination of parameters for the model. Various other Python extensions used were Pandas and Matplotlib for simple data wrangling and visualization.
